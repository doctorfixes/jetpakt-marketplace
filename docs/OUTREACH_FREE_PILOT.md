# Free Limited-Pilot Outreach Email

Offers a no-cost, tailored working version of the ReviewSentinel™ Experience
Enhancement Guide — one guide per restaurant, scoped to their specific recent
guest feedback. Replaces the harder sales ask in the default template.

Use this when the prospect is **cold**, you want to **lower reply friction**,
and you're comfortable investing ~2 hours of build time per respondent.

Defamation-safe guardrails from `GUARDRAILS.md` still apply: verbatim-only
quotes, no staff named, legal-flagged buckets routed to human review.

---

## Subject-line variations (rotate)

1. `A free Experience Enhancement read for {name} — no strings`
2. `Built you a short sample — tell me what to sharpen`
3. `{name}: one free guide, tailored to your top guest signals`
4. `15 minutes of your input → a tailored guide for {name}`

---

## Email body

```
Subject: A free Experience Enhancement read for {name} — no strings

Hi there,

I've been studying how Denver restaurants are turning recent Google and
Yelp signals into operational wins — not by burying reviews, but by
closing the loop on the two or three things guests keep asking for.

I'd like to offer {name} a free, limited working version of what I do —
a tailored one-page Experience Enhancement read, built from your own
recent public reviews. No sales pitch attached. If it's useful, we can
talk about what a fuller engagement looks like. If not, you keep the
guide.

Here's what you'd get, free:

  • A ranked read of the top 3 guest-experience signals showing up in
    your last 30 days of Google and Yelp reviews
  • Each signal tied to verbatim public quotes — nothing invented,
    nothing paraphrased
  • One drafted, defamation-safe response template per signal that your
    team can use under your approval
  • A single-page PDF you can share with your GM or ownership group

To make it genuinely useful, I need 2-3 minutes of your input. Could
you reply with the one or two areas where {name} is feeling the most
pressure right now? A few common ones I hear from Denver operators:

  1. Service pacing at peak hours (ticket times, host handling, table turns)
  2. Billing / service-charge transparency (especially the 15-22% fee
     conversations happening across the city)
  3. Guest-recovery after a bad review — what to say, how fast, by whom
  4. Reservation and waitlist friction (special occasions, walk-ins,
     wait-area experience)
  5. Consistency across shifts — the "Tuesday feels different from
     Saturday" problem
  6. Online review response cadence — volume is outpacing the team
  7. Staff training on specific guest-experience moments (allergies,
     birthdays, large parties)

Just a quick reply — even one sentence — and I'll build the guide
around the area that matters most to you. You'll have it in 48 hours.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Contact]
[Your LinkedIn or website]

---
This outreach is based entirely on public Google, Yelp, and Tripadvisor
review data. No private data, no employee reviews, no scraping behind
authentication. Source URLs available on request.
```

## What to do with the reply

1. Tag the lead in CRM with the pain area they named.
2. Build the tailored one-pager (2-hour build using the ReviewSentinel
   pilot methodology — cover, one prioritized opportunity, verbatim
   excerpts, drafted response template, closing note).
3. Send the PDF within 48 hours of their reply.
4. Three days after send, follow up with a short "what resonated, what
   didn't" question. That's the warm entry point for a paid engagement.

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't send the guide before they reply — the whole point is to get
  them to self-identify the pain area.
- ❌ Don't name staff or quote reviews that mention people by name.
- ❌ Don't reference specific 1-star review URLs.
- ❌ Don't use this variation when the Legal Review Flag is HIGH. For
  service-fee/billing-flagged leads, use the softer variation in
  `OUTREACH_TEMPLATES.md` and have counsel review before send.
- ❌ Don't auto-send. Human-approval gate stays in place.
```
