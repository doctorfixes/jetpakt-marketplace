# Outreach Templates

Three subject-line variations + one master body template, all with
defamation-safe guardrails applied.

## Subject lines (rotated via `subject_idx`)

1. `Denver guests are talking about {name} — a quick idea`
2. `Noticed some recent Google feedback at {name}`
3. `One quick thought on {name}'s recent review trends`

## Body (default — direct reputation-mgmt pitch)

```
Hi there,

I'm reaching out to the management team at {name}. I help Denver-area
restaurants turn recent guest feedback into measurable operational wins —
not by burying reviews, but by closing the loop on the two or three signals
guests are actually asking you to address.

A quick look at {name}'s recent Google and Yelp activity surfaces one
consistent theme: **{key_complaint}**. That's showing up in roughly {neg_pct}%
of your last 30 days of reviews, which is well above the Denver market baseline.

A few things I'd offer — quickly and without a long sales pitch:

  1. A one-page Experience Enhancement read of the top 3 signals, each tied
to verbatim public reviews (nothing invented).
  2. Drafted, defamation-safe response templates your team can send after
every review — approved by you before going out.
  3. A 30-day tracking plan so you can see the complaint share move.

I'd love 15 minutes on the phone. Would {day_slot} work, or would you
prefer I send a 2-page brief first?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Contact]

---
This outreach is based entirely on public Google, Yelp, and Tripadvisor
review data. No private or employee data was used. Full source links
available on request.
```

## Variation — for LEGAL-flagged leads (service-fee / billing)

When `Legal Review Flag` is populated (HIGH for service-fee, MEDIUM for
food safety), **do not use the default body**. A human should hand-write
using this softer template:

```
Hi there,

I'm reaching out to the management team at {name}. I work with Denver
restaurants on guest-experience improvement, using only public review
data — not employee data, not private information.

I'd like to offer a short, no-obligation read of the top 3 recurring
themes from your recent Google and Yelp reviews. No list of complaints,
no shaming — just a concise pattern summary your team can act on.

Open to a 15-minute call?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

Rationale: the soft version avoids referencing specific fee/billing
language in cold outreach, which is critical given the active Denver
CCG service-fee lawsuit.

## Variation — when guests flag server-attitude issues

Replace the complaint line with a more neutral framing:

> That's showing up in roughly {neg_pct}% of recent reviews as
> **inconsistent guest-facing experience**.

Never reference specific staff behavior in the outreach. That invites
employment/defamation issues.

## Follow-up cadence (suggested)

- Day 0: Initial email (subject variation 1)
- Day 3: Short follow-up (subject variation 2)
- Day 7: LinkedIn message to owner/GM (optional, requires Apollo enrichment)
- Day 14: Final send (subject variation 3 — "one more thought")
- Day 30+: Remove from sequence

## What NOT to put in outreach

- ❌ Specific review URLs
- ❌ Named staff / management / servers
- ❌ "I saw a 1-star review that said..."
- ❌ Health / illness / food-poisoning language, even if quoted
- ❌ Mentions of employee lawsuits or labor allegations
- ❌ Any promise to "remove" or "bury" reviews
- ❌ Guarantees of star-rating improvement
